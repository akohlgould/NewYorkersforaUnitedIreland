What Just Happened

The Mayor Is Already Halfway There

On March 17, 2026, Mayor Zohran Mamdani stood at the St. Patrick's Day Parade and, pressed by reporters on Irish unity, declared his belief in "the principle of self-determination" for the Irish people. Then he went home and posted a sweeping, passionate video celebrating 1,600 years of Irish resistance to British colonialism.

"It is no small act to weep with those who weep. It is a choice, one that many do not make."
— Mayor Mamdani, Gracie Mansion St. Patrick's Day Breakfast, March 17, 2026

That same morning, he had quoted James Connolly — "The cause of labor is the cause of Ireland, the cause of Ireland is the cause of labor" — at the James Connolly Irish American Labor Coalition luncheon. He praised Ireland's long tradition of solidarity: with anti-apartheid movements, with Palestinians, with the colonized everywhere.

His labor ally, Transport Workers Union President John Samuelsen, put it plainly: "It's clear to me that he will support the cause for Irish reunification. We're gonna go over this with him in detail, and he's gonna stand with us."

We are asking the Mayor to take the final step: from principle to endorsement. From "self-determination" to "United Ireland." New York is ready. History is ready. Is he?

Historical Context

The Long Arc of Irish Freedom

1845–52

The Great Famine

British colonial mismanagement during the potato famine kills one million Irish and drives another million to New York City. Irish America is born of catastrophe and colonial neglect.

1916

The Easter Rising

James Connolly, Patrick Pearse and their comrades proclaim an Irish Republic. Connolly, both labor organizer and republican revolutionary, is executed by the British. Mayor Mamdani quoted him in March 2026.

1921

Partition Imposed

Britain partitions Ireland, separating six northern counties into "Northern Ireland" against the democratic will of the majority of the Irish people. The wound that still remains open.

1969–98

The Troubles

Thirty years of conflict in Northern Ireland. Catholic and nationalist communities face systematic discrimination, internment without trial, and political exclusion. Ten men die on hunger strike in 1981 — a name-checked by Mamdani in his St. Patrick's Day address.

1998

Good Friday Agreement

Brokered with critical Irish-American involvement, the Agreement ends the armed conflict and — crucially — enshrines the right of the Irish people to vote on reunification in a border poll.

2022

Sinn Féin Wins Stormont

For the first time in history, Sinn Féin becomes the largest party in Northern Ireland's assembly. Michelle O'Neill becomes the first Irish nationalist First Minister. The demographic and political tide has turned.

2026

The Moment Is Now — And the Mayor Is With Us

Mayor Mamdani quotes Connolly, hosts Sinn Féin, addresses 1,600 years of Irish history, and declares belief in self-determination for the Irish. Sinn Féin projects a border poll within four years. New York must speak now.

What We're Asking

Three Steps, Mayor Mamdani.

You've already said you believe in self-determination for the Irish. You've quoted Connolly. You've honored Ireland's resistance. Now we ask you to put the full weight of New York City's voice behind the cause.

1

Issue a Formal Endorsement

Go beyond "self-determination as a principle" and issue a clear, formal mayoral statement endorsing the Irish people's right to a border poll on reunification under the Good Friday Agreement. Words matter. New York's mayors have shaped this debate before. Ed Koch did it. Bill de Blasio did it. Now you.

2

Convene NYC's Irish-American Leadership

Bring together the Transport Workers Union, the Ancient Order of Hibernians, Sinn Féin's American offices, and Irish-American community leaders at City Hall for a formal dialogue on reunification. Make New York a deliberate, organized voice in this democratic conversation — not just a backdrop for a parade.

3

Oppose Westminster's Obstruction

In any international forum, bilateral meeting, or public address, call on the British government to honor the Good Friday Agreement and not obstruct the exercise of a legitimate border poll when the conditions are met. The agreement is international law. You said this city believes in international law. Prove it.
